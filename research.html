Northeastern University - Prof. Juner Zhu's group

Jun 2025 – Present

At Northeastern, my research focuses on designing interpretable machine learning frameworks for material characterization. I developed a physics-informed neural network (ICNN) with quadratic strain energy constraints — ensuring energy positivity, symmetry, and positive definiteness. This framework achieved 98% R² accuracy on finite-element (FEM) strain–stress datasets.

To improve interpretability, I integrated dimensionality reduction and symbolic regression (PySR), extracting closed-form stress–strain relationships and stiffness constants (C11, C12, C22, C66) consistent with classical elasticity. Current efforts extend toward plasticity modeling, leveraging RNN/LSTM architectures to capture path-dependent material responses.

Multi-Physics Modeling

Led large-scale CFD simulations on high-performance computing clusters to generate training data for AI-based Reduced Order Models (ROMs). This work reduced simulation runtime from 6 hours to just 20 seconds, enabling real-time integration into system-level workflows.

Demonstrated a proof of concept on a solenoid valve, which secured $80K in funding to mature the project. The approach leveraged high-fidelity CFD models to train dynamic ROMs. Additionally, I applied workflows to Airbus A320 Oxygen Systems, developing algorithms with NeuralODEs and Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), benchmarking them against digital twin tools for ROM generation.

Surrogate Modeling & Optimization

Developed surrogate models for Air Management Systems (MA700, Boeing 777X), achieving 95% R² accuracy and reducing model compilation time by 60%. Applied advanced regression methods with automated hyperparameter tuning via Bayesian optimization.

Incorporated Uncertainty Quantification (KNN, MCMC) into ROM predictions, integrating Python algorithms into MATLAB with an interactive GUI. The matured solution is now deployed in production workflows at Collins, enabling faster and more reliable plant model development.

Recognized with 2 trade secrets and two Star Awards for delivering innovative solutions and advancing surrogate modeling capabilities.

Reduced Order Modeling of a Heat Exchanger with a Stacking Ensemble

IEEE International Symposium on Systems Engineering (ISSE), 2022

Published and presented at IEEE ISSE 2022, this work proposed a stacking ensemble framework for Reduced Order Modeling (ROM) of a cross-flow heat exchanger to address computational inefficiencies in simulation.

The model combined Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) with a Genetic Programming meta-regressor, achieving R² > 95% compared to ~85–90% for conventional ensemble models. The ROM captured key input–output relationships (inlet temperatures, pressures, and flow rates → outlet temperatures and pressures) while reducing Simulink simulation runtime from minutes to seconds.

A major advantage of this framework is its black-box deployability, allowing industrial collaborators to use reduced models without exposing proprietary system design details.
