Physics-Informed Machine Learning for Constitutive Modeling

Northeastern University — Prof. Juner Zhu’s Group

At Northeastern, my current research focuses on the development of interpretable constitutive models using physics-informed machine learning. The primary goal is to create models that not only predict mechanical responses but are also interpretable and provide physical insight.

Ongoing efforts explore trainability on time-varying experimental data, embedding physical constraints into architectures like PINNs and ICNNs, and balancing predictive accuracy with interpretability of constitutive laws.

Reduced Order Models & Surrogate Optimization

Collins Aerospace — R&D Internship

At Collins Aerospace, my work spanned two key projects:

  • Reduced Order Models (ROMs): Developed multi-fidelity ROMs for aerospace components to reduce computational expense in CFD simulations.
  • Surrogate Modeling: Built machine learning-based surrogate models for rapid design exploration and optimization, enabling efficient trade-off analysis across operating conditions.

These projects highlighted the synergy between physics-based simulation and machine learning models for aerospace design acceleration.

Reduced Order Modeling of a Heat Exchanger with a Stacking Ensemble

IEEE ISSE Conference — IIT Guwahati

This publication presented a stacking ensemble method for reduced order modeling of a heat exchanger, addressing computational inefficiencies in system simulations. The approach combined multiple surrogate learners to improve accuracy and generalizability over traditional ROMs.

Key contributions included:

  • Demonstrated ensemble surrogates outperforming single-model ROMs.
  • Validated performance on experimental heat exchanger datasets.
  • Showed significant reduction in computational cost with high accuracy.
