CFD — Aerodynamics of DrivAer Models in a Platoon (OpenFOAM)

I simulated the Notchback DrivAer model (single car and two-car platoon) in OpenFOAM v8 to study how spacing and speed affect drag and wake structures. The setup was viscous, incompressible, RANS with a two-equation k–ω model; I validated turbulence modeling on NASA’s backward-facing-step case and also validated the methodology on the Ahmed body before moving to DrivAer. For the platoon, I ran 30 m/s and 40 m/s across spacing ratios s = 0.25–1.5 (normalized by vehicle length).

Key Visuals

Method & Validation

  • Solver: simpleFoam (steady, incompressible, turbulent) with SIMPLE; potentialFoam used to initialize the pressure field.
  • Turbulence: k–ω / k–ω SST considered; k–ω showed best agreement on re-attachment length in the NASA backward-facing-step benchmark, so I used it for subsequent studies.
  • Ahmed body check: slant 25°, Re≈2.76×10⁶ at 40 m/s. Present-study Cd ≈ 0.311, consistent with literature.
  • Single DrivAer (Notchback): present-study Cd ≈ 0.408.

Platoon Findings

  • At s = 0.25 L, drag dropped by ~24% (30 m/s) and ~32% (40 m/s).
  • Rear-vehicle influence significant up to 0.75 L (~3.42 m).
  • At 40 m/s, both cars show greater drag reduction due to stronger pressure recovery between vehicles.

Talk / Recognition

Presented at Research & Industrial Conclave 2022, IIT Guwahati — “A Numerical Investigation on the Aerodynamics of Notchback DrivAer Model in a Platoon.”
