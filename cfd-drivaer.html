CFD — Aerodynamics of DrivAer Models in a Platoon (OpenFOAM)

I simulated the Notchback DrivAer model (single car and two-car platoon) in OpenFOAM v8 to study how spacing and speed affect drag and wake structures. The setup was viscous, incompressible, RANS with a two-equation k–ω model; I validated turbulence modeling on NASA’s backward-facing-step case and also validated the methodology on the Ahmed body before moving to DrivAer. For the platoon, I ran 30 m/s and 40 m/s across spacing ratios s = 0.25–1.5 (normalized by vehicle length).

Method & Validation

  • Solver: simpleFoam (steady, incompressible, turbulent) with SIMPLE; potentialFoam used to initialize the pressure field.
  • Turbulence: k–ω / k–ω SST considered; k–ω showed best agreement on re-attachment length in the NASA backward-facing-step benchmark, so I used it for subsequent studies.
  • Ahmed body check: slant 25°, Re≈2.76×10⁶ at 40 m/s. Present-study Cd ≈ 0.311, consistent with literature.
  • Single DrivAer (Notchback): present-study Cd ≈ 0.408; clear stagnation at the nose and massive rear wake.

Platoon Setup (2 vehicles)

  • Domain ~44 m × 8 m × 6.4 m; speeds 30 and 40 m/s; spacing s = 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 1.5 (normalized by car length).
  • Mesh: snappyHexMesh with refinement boxes; ~5.06 M nodes / 4.71 M cells at s=0.25; 30 layers for boundary layer resolution.

Key Findings

  • Drag vs spacing: At s=0.25 L, drag dropped by ~24% (30 m/s) and ~32% (40 m/s). Benefits diminish as spacing increases; rear-vehicle influence is significant up to about 0.75 L (~3.42 m).
  • Trend: With increasing spacing, the leading-vehicle drag rises toward the single-car baseline, while the trailing car’s drag approaches its single-car value from below; overall normalized Cd increases with spacing.
  • Speed effect: At 40 m/s, both cars show better fuel-economy gains versus 30 m/s because stronger stagnation pressure on the lead car raises the pressure between cars, reducing pressure differential and thus drag on the lead vehicle.

Tools

OpenFOAM v8 (simpleFoam, snappyHexMesh), k–ω / k–ω SST turbulence modeling, Linux/HPC environment.

Talk / Recognition

Presented at Research & Industrial Conclave 2022, IIT Guwahati — “A Numerical Investigation on the Aerodynamics of Notchback DrivAer Model in a Platoon.”
